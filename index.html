THE DISAPPEARING ARTS

"Traditional Rwandan art forms like Imigongo paintings and Intore dance are vanishing faster than we can document them. Each elder we lose takes irreplaceable knowledge with them."

â€” Niyonkuru, 72, Imigongo Master

Vanishing Traditions

Imigongo and Intore are disappearing in rural districts (Huye, Musanze, Nyagatare) due to modernization and youth disengagement.

Economic Impact

Cultural tourism generated $620M in 2023 (36% growth), but declining art forms threaten this revenue stream.

Oral Tradition Crisis

With knowledge passed orally, we've lost 60% of master artisans in the last decade without proper documentation.

OUR HYBRID SOLUTION

Combining digital preservation with community empowerment

Digital Archive

Comprehensive documentation of 5 endangered art forms with 3D scanning and oral history recordings.

  • High-resolution photography
  • Step-by-step creation videos
  • Interactive learning modules

Mobile Workshops

Traveling teams conducting hands-on training in Huye, Musanze, and Nyagatare districts.

  • Elder-youth apprenticeship programs
  • Traditional materials sourcing
  • Community cultural festivals

Economic Pathways

Creating sustainable income through cultural tourism and e-commerce platforms.

  • Artisan cooperatives
  • Digital marketplace
  • Cultural experience tours

LESSONS & VISION

Our journey and roadmap for Rwanda's cultural future

Key Learnings

1

Intergenerational Bridges

Pairing youth with elders through digital tools created unexpected bonds and better knowledge transfer.

2

Modern-Traditional Fusion

Youth engagement tripled when we incorporated contemporary designs into traditional art forms.

3

Economic Incentives

Creating visible income opportunities became our most effective recruitment tool.

Future Goals

Cultural Hubs

Establish permanent centers in Huye, Musanze, and Nyagatare by 2025 serving 500+ artisans annually.

Global Marketplace

Launch e-commerce platform connecting Rwandan artisans with international buyers.

School Curriculum

Partner with MINEDUC to integrate traditional arts into national education by 2026.
